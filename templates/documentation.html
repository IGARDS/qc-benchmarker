Documentation


QC Benchmarker relies on using data generated from a commercial protein digest, and that the data be acquired in a standardized fashion. The following describes what materials are supported, how to prepare them, and LC-MS/MS settings for analysis.

Commercial Digest

While still in beta, only a HeLa digest is supported by QC Benchmarker, but in the future the following will be supported:

Thermo Scientific HeLa Digest

Promega Yeast Digest

Waters E. coli Digest


Loading and LC Conditions

The recommended loading is 200 ng on column. Prepare the material using the manufacturer's suggested protocol.

The recommended LC gradient for HeLa and yeast is:

Time (min) % Acetonitrile
0 5
10 5
115 22
130 32
140 95

150.1 5
160 5
(if using a trap/elute set-up, the first step is 10 minutes of trapping)

There will be a shorter gradient used for E. coli or for ~40 ng loading of HeLa and yeast.

Miscellaneous

It is important to note that system performance can vary, and that QC Benchmarker is meant to describe at what performance has dropped precipitously. Thresholds are based on typical runs for each platform (not outlier excellent runs), but this isnâ€™t to say that these thresholds are universally applicable. For example, LC peak widths may vary from 15 seconds to 60 seconds across labs. If 60 seconds is considered normal, then any quality judgement QC Benchmarker makes should be ignored. But the concept of running a single material under standard conditions will give users the ability to generate quality metrics over time, and thereby inform system performance and direct troubleshooting.
